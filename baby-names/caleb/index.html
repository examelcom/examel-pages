About Caleb

Caleb has a lovely, solid feel—there's warmth in those two syllables that rolls off the tongue naturally. It strikes a nice balance between classic and contemporary, never feeling trendy or dated. The name carries a gentle strength, with an easygoing rhythm that suits both a curious toddler and a confident adult.

The nickname potential is great too. You could go with Cal for something casual and cool, or Cale if you want a slight variation. There's also the option to use the full name, which sounds complete and substantial on its own.

There's something approachable about Caleb—it feels like the kind of name that belongs to someone friendly and reliable, someone people naturally want to know. It's popular enough that your son won't be the only one, but not so trendy that it feels like everyone has that name. It's simply a name that works well across different ages and stages of life.

According to SSA data, Caleb has been given to 315,583 babies since 1975. Its most popular year was 2002 with 11,665 births.
