About Cooper

Cooper has a friendly, approachable feel that's become increasingly popular with modern parents. It's got a crisp, two-syllable rhythm that's easy to say and remember, with a casual warmth that doesn't feel stuffy. The name carries a gentle strength—there's something both wholesome and adventurous about it.

What makes Cooper special is its versatility. It works equally well on a toddler, a teenager, and a grown man, adapting naturally across different life stages. The name evokes someone capable and reliable, yet approachable—think someone who's equally comfortable in a boardroom or building something with their hands. Common nicknames like Coop keep things playful and relaxed.

This is a name that's reached genuine popularity for good reason. It feels contemporary without trying too hard, and it has that rare quality of being both trendy and timeless.

According to SSA data, Cooper has been given to 115,174 babies since 1975. Its most popular year was 2024 with 5,699 births.
