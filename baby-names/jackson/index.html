About Jackson

Jackson has such a friendly, approachable feel—it's a name that works equally well on a little boy and a grown man. There's a classic strength to it without being stuffy. The nickname Jack is practically built in, giving your son options as he grows.

The name strikes a nice balance between timeless and contemporary. It has that easy, confident vibe—think of someone who's both dependable and fun to be around. Jackson rolls off the tongue smoothly, with a natural rhythm that feels warm rather than formal.

Parents seem to love Jackson because it's familiar enough to feel grounded, but not so common that it feels overused. It's the kind of name that feels like a genuine choice rather than a trend, even though it's been a favorite for years.

According to SSA data, Jackson has been given to 279,263 babies since 1975. Its most popular year was 2013 with 12,609 births.
